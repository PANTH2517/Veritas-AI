SYSTEM DESIGN
SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE: WHAT DOES WHAT?

Veritas AI operates on a multi-layered hybrid architecture combining deep learning classifiers with hand-crafted digital media forensics:

  • 1. MobileNetV2 CNN Classifier (86% Decision Weight) Extracts deep latent feature embeddings (1280-dimensional vectors) fine-tuned on 140,000 high-resolution real and GAN-generated face images. It operates at an 86% decision weight in standard image scans, targeting semantic anomalies, GAN fingerprints, and generative textures.
  • 2. Forensic Signal MLP (14% Decision Weight) Processes 22 handcrafted digital forensic metrics (LBP entropy, contour smoothness, specularity highlights, boundary gradients, FFT ring metrics, etc.). The MLP evaluates low-level sensor artifacts and pixel-level anomalies at a 14% decision weight.
  • 3. Hybrid Fusion & Calibration Engine Dynamically integrates predictions from both the deep network and the hand-crafted forensic parser to compute the final combined risk probability, which is then compared against the active decision boundary.
  • 4. Voice Deepfake Spectrogram Analyzer (Audio/Video) For files containing audio, a dedicated acoustic forensic pipeline extracts high-frequency energy, phase coherence, spectral centroid/flux, and bandwidth anomaly metrics to flag synthetic speech.
THRESHOLD CALIBRATION RATIONALE

Separating overlapping feature distributions between organic captures and synthetic media requires a calibrated decision boundary:

  • The Overlap Problem (Camera Noise & compression) Real-world organic images are rarely "perfectly clean." Camera sensor noise, jpeg compression blocking, shadows, and lens artifacts can trigger low-level forensic anomalies. As a result, authentic images can score up to 93% on raw anomaly detectors.
  • Why We Calibrate (Distribution Separability) Because real and AI distribution curves overlap, a hard threshold is required to divide benign sensor noise from actual neural fabrication. Setting the default threshold to 93.5% optimizes the classification boundary for general web media.
  • Tuning Precision vs. Recall (Operational Trade-offs) Adjusting the threshold allows operators to fine-tune the detector's sensitivity:
    • Strict Threshold (Higher, > 95%): Focuses on high precision, minimizing False Positives (real files flagged as fakes). Ideal for content platforms where incorrect censorship is costly.
    • Sensitive Threshold (Lower, < 90%): Focuses on high recall, capturing even the most subtle, blended, or compressed deepfakes, at the cost of higher False Positives. Ideal for high-security environments, legal verification, or intelligence gathering.
